GROUP B RALLY

THE GOLDEN ERA OF RALLY/ 1982-1986

The Legend

Tight roads, insane crowds, no regulations & 600 horsepower monsters. Welcome to Group B.

Group B was a legendary four-year period from 1982 to 1986 when rallying became a "technological arms race." Minimal regulations meant manufacturers could race experimental prototypes on public roads.

  • Engineering Freedom:

    With no limits on turbocharger boost and few restrictions on materials, cars like the Audi Sport Quattro S1 reached nearly 700 horsepower.

  • Insane Crowds:

    Millions of spectators lined the tracks, often standing directly on the road as cars approached at triple-digit speeds.

  • Rapid Innovation:

    This era pioneered all-wheel-drive (Audi Quattro) and "twin-charging" (Lancia Delta S4), which used both a turbo and a supercharger to eliminate engine lag.

The era ultimately ended in 1987 when the machines became too fast for human reaction time, leading to a series of high-profile fatal accidents.


"WRC is for boys. Group B was for men."
— Juha Kankkunen, 1986 World Rally Champion

The Machines

  • Audi Quattro S1

    The car that pioneered the four-wheel-drive system.

    • Legendary 5-cylinder turbocharged engine.

  • Lancia Delta S4

    One of the most extreme cars of the era.

    • Unique "twin-charging" system (Turbo + Supercharger).

  • Ford RS200

    Ford's purpose-built Group B contender.

    • Equipped with a 1.8L Cosworth turbocharged engine.
    • One of the rarest Group B cars, with only 200 units ever produced.

The Icons

It took a special kind of driver to handle 500+ horsepower on narrow, dirt-filled mountain roads. These drivers were icons of the sport who pushed the very limits.

  • Juha Kankkunen

    • The only driver to win the Group B title in its final year (1986).
    • The man who mastered the Peugeot 205 T16

  • Walter Röhrl

    • The "Genius" behind the Audi Quattro.
    • Known for his incredible technical precision and left-foot braking.

  • Michèle Mouton

    • The most successful female rally driver in history.
    • Runner-up in the 1982 World Championship driving for Audi.
